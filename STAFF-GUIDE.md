# Editing the Rosehill College website — staff guide

A plain-English guide to updating the website. No code, no technical knowledge
needed. If you can fill in a form, you can edit the site.

---

## What you can do

- Change any **text** on any page
- Swap or upload **photos**
- Update **term dates, contact details, news, staff lists, documents**
- Add, remove and reorder the **sections** (blocks) that make up a page

## What you can't do (on purpose)

You look after the **content**, not the design. So you can't accidentally:

- change colours, fonts, spacing or the homepage animation
- create or delete whole pages, or change a page's web address
- break how the site looks on phones

The design stays locked, so the site always looks right no matter what you type.

---

## 1. Getting in

1. Go to **https://rosehill-college-website.pages.dev/admin/**
2. Click **Login with GitHub**
3. If it asks, click **Authorize** (first time only on each computer)

You'll land on the editor home screen with two areas:

- **Site settings** — things that appear on *every* page: phone numbers, email,
  addresses, the top menu, footer links, social links.
- **Pages** — every page on the site, grouped by section (Our School,
  Curriculum, Join Us, etc.).

> **Tip:** Bookmark that `/admin/` address so you can find it again.

---

## 2. The urgent notice banner 🔔

A coloured band across the **top of the homepage** — for closures, event changes
and anything parents must see the moment they land on the site.

1. Go to **Site settings** → open **🔔 Urgent notice banner (homepage)**.
2. Turn **Show the banner** on.
3. Pick a **Level** — *Urgent* (red), *Important* (gold) or *Notice* (navy).
   This only sets the colour.
4. Type the **Message** — keep it to one or two sentences.
5. *(Optional)* add a **link** (e.g. *Read more* → the news page) and an
   **auto-hide date** so the banner takes itself down.
6. **Publish → Publish now.** It's live in 1–2 minutes.

**To take the banner down:** go back to the same place, turn **Show the banner**
off, and Publish. (Or set the auto-hide date and it clears itself.)

> **Good to know:** parents can dismiss the banner with the ✕, and it won't nag
> them again for that same message. Change the wording and it shows once more.

---

## 3. Making an edit

1. Click **Pages**, then the page you want (e.g. *Term Dates*).
2. Each page is a stack of **sections** — labelled blocks like *Hero (page top)*,
   *Table*, *Photo + text*, *Call to action band*. Each shows its heading so you
   can find the right one.
3. Click the **`>` arrow** on a section to open it and see its fields.
4. Edit the fields. Changes save as you go (you'll see *Changes saved*).

### Reordering or removing sections

- **Drag** the `=` handle (top of a section) up or down to reorder.
- Click the **`✕`** to remove a section.
- Click **Add sections** to insert a new block, then pick a type.

### Formatting text

Longer text fields have a toolbar — **bold**, links, bullet lists. You don't
need to know any code; just use the buttons.

---

## 4. Publishing (making it go live)

**Editing is not the same as publishing.** To put your changes on the real
website:

1. Click **Publish** (top-left) → **Publish now**.
2. Wait **1–2 minutes** while the site rebuilds.
3. Click **View Live** (top-right) to see your change on the real page.

That's it. Your change is now live for everyone.

> **If you don't click Publish, nothing goes live.** So you can safely explore
> and only publish when you're ready.

---

## 5. Photos

- Any photo field has a **Photo** button — choose an existing photo from the
  library, or **upload** a new one from your computer.
- Always fill in the **Alt text** box: a short description of what's in the photo
  (e.g. "Students performing kapa haka"). This is read aloud to people using a
  screen reader, and helps Google. Leave it blank only if the image is purely
  decorative.
- **Big photos are fine** — the site automatically shrinks them to the right
  sizes when you publish. Upload straight off a phone or camera.
- **Faces and grounds must be real Rosehill photos.** Don't use stock images of
  students or staff.

## 6. Common tasks — quick reference

| I want to… | Go to |
| --- | --- |
| Put up an urgent notice (closure, alert) | **Site settings** → 🔔 Urgent notice banner |
| Change the school phone number or email | **Site settings** → Contact |

| Update term dates or bell times | **Pages → Term Dates** → the *Table* sections |

| Update enrolment dates | **Pages → Enrolment** |

## 7. Good habits & things to watch

- **Macrons matter.** Write te reo Māori correctly — *whānau*, *Māori*,
  *Ngāti Tamaoho*. Copy-paste from a reliable source if unsure.
- **"Search-engine description" is invisible.** It doesn't show on the page —
  it's the summary Google displays in search results. Editing it won't change
  anything you can see on the page itself. That's normal.
- **Links:** for a page on our own site, use the address without `.html`
  (e.g. `/join-us/enrolment`). For another website, paste the full `https://…`
  address and tick "Links to another website".
- **Check it after publishing.** Always click **View Live** and glance at the
  real page — that's the true preview.
- **One change at a time** when you're learning. Edit, publish, check, repeat.

---

## 8. If something looks wrong

- **The editor won't load / won't log in** — try a hard refresh
  (Ctrl+Shift+R), or a different browser. If it persists, contact IT.
- **My change isn't showing** — did you click **Publish → Publish now**? And
  did you wait 1–2 minutes? Try refreshing the live page (Ctrl+Shift+R).
- **I made a mistake and published it** — don't panic. Every change is saved in
  history and can be undone. Contact IT to roll it back.
- **A photo looks squashed or badly cropped** — tell IT which page; there's a
  quick fix for the crop position.

---

## The golden rule

**Edit → Publish → View Live → check.**

If you can see it on the live page and it looks right, you're done. If it
doesn't look right, you can always change it again — you can't break the design.
