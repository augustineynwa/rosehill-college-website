Editing the Rosehill College website

Staff guide · no code or technical knowledge needed

If you can fill in a form, you can edit the site. You look after the content — the words, photos and documents. The design stays locked, so the site always looks right no matter what you type.

✔ You can

  • Change any text on any page
  • Swap or upload photos
  • Update term dates, contacts, news, staff, documents
  • Add, remove and reorder sections

✘ You can't (on purpose)

  • Change colours, fonts or spacing
  • Create or delete whole pages, or change a web address
  • Break how the site looks on phones

1Getting in

  1. Go to rosehill-college-website.pages.dev/admin/
  2. Click Login with GitHub
  3. If asked, click Authorize (first time only, on each computer)

You'll see two areas:

  • Site settings — things on every page: phone numbers, email, addresses, the top menu, footer links, socials.
  • Pages — every page, grouped by section (Our School, Curriculum, Join Us…).
Tip: bookmark the /admin/ address so it's easy to find again.

2The urgent notice banner 🔔

A red/gold/navy band across the top of the homepage — for closures, event changes and anything parents must see the moment they land on the site.

  1. Go to Site settings → open 🔔 Urgent notice banner (homepage).
  2. Turn Show the banner on.
  3. Pick a Level — Urgent (red), Important (gold) or Notice (navy). This just sets the colour.
  4. Type the Message — keep it to one or two sentences.
  5. (Optional) add a link (e.g. Read more → the news page), and an auto-hide date so it takes itself down.
  6. Publish → Publish now. It's live in 1–2 minutes.
To take the banner down: go back to the same place, turn Show the banner off, and Publish. (Or set the auto-hide date and it clears itself.)
Good to know: parents can dismiss it with the ✕, and it won't nag them again for that same message. Change the wording and it shows once more.

3Making an edit

  1. Click Pages, then the page you want (e.g. Term Dates).
  2. A page is a stack of sections — labelled blocks like Hero, Table, Photo + text. Each shows its heading so you can find the right one.
  3. Click the > arrow on a section to open it and see its fields.
  4. Edit the fields. Changes save as you go.

Reorder or remove: drag the = handle to move a section; click ✕ to remove one; click Add sections to insert a new block.

Formatting: longer text fields have a toolbar for bold, links and bullet lists — just use the buttons, no code needed.

4Publishing — making it go live

Editing is not the same as publishing. Nothing appears on the real website until you publish.
  1. Click Publish (top-left) → Publish now.
  2. Wait 1–2 minutes while the site rebuilds.
  3. Click View Live (top-right) to see your change on the real page.

That's it — your change is now live for everyone. You can safely explore and only publish when you're ready.

5Photos

  • Use the Photo button to pick an existing photo or upload a new one.
  • Always fill in Alt text — a short description of what's in the photo (e.g. "Students performing kapa haka"). It's read aloud to people using a screen reader.
  • Big photos are fine — the site shrinks them automatically when you publish. Upload straight off a phone or camera.
  • Real Rosehill photos only — never stock images of students or staff.

7Good habits & things to watch

  • Macrons matter. Write te reo Māori correctly — whānau, Māori, Ngāti Tamaoho. Copy-paste from a reliable source if unsure.
  • "Search-engine description" is invisible. It doesn't show on the page — it's the summary Google displays in search results. Editing it won't change anything you can see.
  • Links: for a page on our own site, use the address without .html (e.g. /join-us/enrolment). For another website, paste the full https://… address and tick "Links to another website".
  • Always check after publishing. Click View Live and glance at the real page — that's the true preview.

8If something looks wrong

  • Won't load or log in — hard refresh (Ctrl+Shift+R) or try another browser. If it persists, contact IT.
  • My change isn't showing — did you Publish → Publish now, and wait 1–2 minutes? Refresh the live page (Ctrl+Shift+R).
  • I published a mistake — don't panic. Every change is saved in history and can be undone. Contact IT to roll it back.
  • A photo looks squashed or badly cropped — tell IT which page; there's a quick fix.
The golden rule
Edit → Publish → View Live → check
If it looks right on the live page, you're done. If not, change it again — you can't break the design.
